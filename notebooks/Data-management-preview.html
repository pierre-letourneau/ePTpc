Longitudinal evaluation of ePTpc and GFR slope association

GFR decline is the critical outcome in Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) progression. This analysis investigates the association between a patient’s baseline ePTpc and the subsequent long-term GFR decline rate (slope).

We utilize a Linear Mixed-Effects Model (LMM) to appropriately handle the correlated nature of longitudinal GFR measurements. The model’s primary focus is the interaction term between the follow-up time and baseline ePTpc, adjusting for key confounders like baseline GFR and CKD etiology.

Using the counterfactual prediction approach, we graphically illustrate the predicted average GFR trajectories over time for different initial ePTpc levels. This figure will clarify the marginal effect of baseline ePTpc on the speed of GFR deterioration which is non clinically and statistically significant in our model.
